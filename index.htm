About

I am a PhD Candidate at the L3S Research Center, Leibniz Universität Hannover, Germany, where I work under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Wolfgang Nejdl. My research lies at the intersection of multilingual NLP, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and low-resource language modeling, with a particular focus on morphologically complex Ge'ez-script languages — Tigrinya, Amharic, and Classical Ge'ez.

I also serve as a Research Associate at the University of Zurich, collaborating with Prof. Dr. Nesina Grütter on computational linguistics and digital humanities. I hold the DAAD Hilde Domin Scholarship as a Scholar at Risk, and bring over 12 years of experience in research, university teaching, and academic leadership across Ethiopia, China, and Germany.

In 2025, my team secured 3rd place and a $2,000 prize at the SIGIR LiveRAG Challenge, building a full end-to-end RAG pipeline over a 15M-document corpus using Falcon3-10B-Instruct and hybrid retrieval indices.

Research

My overarching research theme is Linguistically Grounded NLP for Multilingual and Low-Resource Settings. I develop methods that integrate morphological and lexical knowledge into large language models and retrieval systems, targeting languages that are structurally complex yet severely underrepresented in NLP benchmarks and infrastructure.

Current Focus

Morphology-Aware Tokenization & Vocabulary Expansion

Developing subword construction methods (MoVoC) and lexically grounded embedding initialization (LGSE) to reduce tokenization disparities and improve LLM access for Ge'ez-script languages.

Active

Retrieval-Augmented Generation Systems

Building and evaluating RAG pipelines with hybrid retrieval, query-to-embedding matching, and large-scale document corpora. Led the RAGtifier system at SIGIR LiveRAG 2025 (3rd place, $2,000 prize).

Active

Computational Resources for Classical Ge'ez

Building NLP datasets, morphological synthesizers, and machine translation benchmarks for Tigrinya and Amharic; affiliated with the University of Zurich for digital humanities applications.
