I am a PhD candidate at the L3S Research Center, Leibniz University Hannover, Germany. My research focuses on multilingual natural language processing, morphology-aware modeling, retrieval-augmented generation, and information retrieval for low-resource and underrepresented languages.

My work aims to develop linguistically informed large language models and scalable AI systems that improve knowledge access, cross-lingual understanding, and equitable technology for diverse global communities.

Previously, I conducted doctoral research at Beijing University of Technology on computational NLP topics including fake news detection and sarcasm detection. I also served as an Assistant Professor at Mekelle University and worked as a Lecturer at the University of Gondar.

I am passionate about inclusive AI, interdisciplinary research, mentoring, and open science.
