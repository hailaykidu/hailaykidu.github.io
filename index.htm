About

I am a PhD Candidate at the L3S Research Center, Leibniz University Hannover, Germany. My research focuses on multilingual Natural Language Processing, Information Retrieval, and morphology-aware modeling for low-resource and underrepresented languages.

My work advances NLP solutions for low-resource languages by developing linguistically informed large language model representations and deploying them effectively in data-limited environments. This contributes to scalable and equitable AI systems that overcome linguistic barriers, improve cross-lingual understanding, and support impactful real-world applications across diverse global contexts.

I previously conducted doctoral research at the Beijing University of Technology, where I worked on computational NLP topics including fake news detection and sarcasm detection. I also served as an Assistant Professor at Mekelle University.

In addition, I worked as a Lecturer at University of Gondar, Ethiopia, and as a Data Analyst at Cybersoft Company, gaining experience in data-driven solutions and applied analytics.

I also continue to work as a Research Associate at the University of Zurich, contributing to interdisciplinary research in computational linguistics and digital humanities.
