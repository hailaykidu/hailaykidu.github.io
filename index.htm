About

I am a PhD Candidate at the L3S Research Center, Leibniz University Hannover , Germany. My research focuses on multilingual Natural Language Processing, Information Retrieval, and morphology-aware modeling for low-resource and underrepresented languages.

My work aims to advance linguistically informed large language models and retrieval-augmented systems that improve knowledge access, cross-lingual understanding, and digital inclusion across diverse global communities.

I previously conducted doctoral research at the Beijing University of Technology , working on computational approaches to fake news detection and sarcasm detection. I also served as an Assistant Professor at Mekelle University and as a Lecturer at the University of Gondar , Ethiopia.

I currently contribute as a Research Associate at the University of Zurich , supporting interdisciplinary research in computational linguistics and digital humanities.

Beyond research, I am passionate about teaching and mentoring students in NLP, IR, and AI. Outside academia, I enjoy cycling and swimming.
