About

I am a PhD candidate at the L3S Research Center, Leibniz University Hannover, Germany. My research focuses on multilingual and low-resource natural language processing, morphology-aware modeling, retrieval-augmented generation, and information retrieval for underrepresented languages.

My work aims to advance linguistically informed large language models and equitable AI systems that improve knowledge access, cross-lingual understanding, and digital inclusion across diverse global communities.

Previously, I conducted doctoral research at Beijing University of Technology on fake news detection and sarcasm detection. I also served as an Assistant Professor at Mekelle University and as a Lecturer at the University of Gondar.

Research Interests

  • Morphology-Aware Natural Language Processing
  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation
  • Information Retrieval
